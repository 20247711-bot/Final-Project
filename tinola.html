Tinola: A Warm Filipino Comfort

A simple, fragrant chicken soup that tells a story of home and tradition.

Tinola, or tinulang manok, has no single known inventor. Its method—simmering meat with ginger and local vegetables—is an ancient Filipino cooking style.

Traditionally, it features chicken, green papaya, and chili leaves, making it a simple yet aromatic dish. Ginger (luya) adds a fragrant warmth to the broth.

Tinola is even mentioned in Jose Rizal's Noli Me Tángere, symbolizing comfort, family, and cultural heritage—a true pre-colonial Filipino dish.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into serving pieces
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 inches fresh ginger, julienned
  • 6 cups water or rice washing
  • 1 small green papaya, cubed
  • 1 cup chili leaves or malunggay
  • Fish sauce and black pepper, to taste

How to Cook It

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil and sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  2. Sear the Chicken: Add chicken and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Simmer: Add water and fish sauce. Simmer 20–30 minutes until chicken is tender.
  4. Add Papaya: Add papaya cubes and cook 5–7 minutes.
  5. Add Greens: Add chili leaves or malunggay and stir gently.
  6. Serve: Adjust seasoning and serve hot with steamed rice.

TUTORIAL


Tip: For richer flavor, sauté the chicken lightly before simmering to enhance the broth.
