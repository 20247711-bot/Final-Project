Bistek Tagalog: Classic Filipino Beef Steak

A savory and tangy Filipino dish made with tender beef, soy sauce, calamansi, and caramelized onions.

Bistek Tagalog, also known as Filipino beef steak, is a well-loved savory dish made with thinly sliced beef marinated in soy sauce and calamansi. It is simmered until tender and topped with sweet, caramelized onions.

This classic Filipino ulam is known for its perfect balance of salty, sour, and slightly sweet flavors. It pairs best with hot steamed rice and is a staple in many Filipino households.

A Hearty Favorite

With its rich sauce and tender beef, Bistek Tagalog continues to be a comforting dish enjoyed by families across generations.

Ingredients

  • 500g beef sirloin or beef top round, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 4–5 pieces calamansi (or 1 lemon), juiced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large onions, sliced into rings
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup water or beef broth
  • Salt to taste

How to Cook It

  1. Marinate the Beef: Combine soy sauce, calamansi juice, garlic, and pepper. Add the sliced beef and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Caramelize the Onions: Heat oil in a pan and sauté the onion rings until soft and lightly browned. Set aside.
  3. Cook the Beef: In the same pan, add the marinated beef (reserve marinade). Sear both sides until lightly browned.
  4. Simmer: Pour the remaining marinade and add water or broth. Cover and simmer on low heat until the beef becomes tender.
  5. Finish: Add the caramelized onions back into the pan. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve hot with steamed rice.

TUTORIAL


Cooking Tip: For extra tenderness, simmer the beef on low heat and avoid rushing the cooking process.
