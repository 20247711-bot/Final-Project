Pinakbet: Filipino Vegetable Stew

A savory stew of mixed vegetables, shrimp paste, and sometimes pork, Pinakbet is a hearty and flavorful Filipino classic.

Pinakbet, also known as *Pakbet*, is a traditional Filipino dish that originated in the Ilocos region of the Philippines. It's a savory vegetable stew featuring a mix of local vegetables such as bitter melon (ampalaya), eggplant, okra, and squash, cooked in shrimp paste (*bagoong*), and sometimes pork or beef for added flavor.

This dish is not only nutritious but also incredibly flavorful due to the combination of earthy vegetables and salty shrimp paste. Pinakbet is typically enjoyed with steamed rice, making it a fulfilling meal.

A Dish from the Heart of Ilocos

Pinakbet reflects the Filipino love for hearty, healthy meals made with simple, fresh ingredients. It’s a dish that brings out the essence of Filipino cuisine — bold, savory, and packed with love.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly or bagnet, sliced (optional)
  • 1 medium bitter melon (ampalaya), sliced
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced into rounds
  • 1 cup okra, sliced into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium squash (kalabasa), cut into cubes
  • 1 large tomato, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong)
  • 1 cup water or vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil for sautéing

How to Cook It

  1. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic, onion, and tomato until softened.
  2. Cook the Pork (Optional): Add pork belly or bagnet, and cook until browned and crispy.
  3. Add Shrimp Paste: Stir in shrimp paste and cook for 2-3 minutes to bring out the flavors.
  4. Add Vegetables: Add squash, eggplant, bitter melon, and okra. Mix gently to combine.
  5. Simmer: Add water or broth, fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the vegetables are tender and the flavors are well combined.
  6. Serve: Adjust seasoning to taste, and serve hot with steamed rice.

TUTORIAL


Cooking Tip: If you want a richer flavor, use fermented shrimp paste and adjust the amount according to your taste preference.
