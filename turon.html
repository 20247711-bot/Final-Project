Turon: Sweet Filipino Snack

A delicious and crunchy Filipino dessert made from sweet bananas, sugar, and spring roll wrappers.

Turon is a beloved Filipino snack that consists of sliced bananas (usually saba), coated with sugar, wrapped in a spring roll wrapper, and deep-fried until golden and crispy. It’s often served as a quick dessert or snack, especially for street vendors in the Philippines.

It’s also known as “banana lumpia” and is often enjoyed with a variety of fillings such as jackfruit or sweetened coconut, but the classic version uses just the banana and sugar.

A Crispy Delight

With its crispy, caramelized exterior and soft, sweet interior, turon has become a favorite street food for Filipinos of all ages.

Ingredients

  • 4 pieces saba bananas (or any sweet variety of banana)
  • 1 cup brown sugar (for coating)
  • Spring roll wrappers (about 8-10 pieces)
  • Jackfruit (optional)
  • Oil for frying

How to Cook It

  1. Prepare the Bananas: Peel and slice the saba bananas into strips. Optionally, you can add jackfruit slices.
  2. Coat with Sugar: Roll each banana strip in brown sugar, ensuring it’s well-coated.
  3. Wrap the Turon: Place a sugar-coated banana strip in the center of a spring roll wrapper. Fold the edges and roll tightly.
  4. Fry the Turon: Heat oil in a pan. Once hot, fry the turon until golden brown and crispy, turning occasionally to ensure even frying.
  5. Serve: Drain excess oil on paper towels and serve hot, optionally with a drizzle of caramelized sugar on top.

TUTORIAL


Cooking Tip: Ensure the oil is hot enough so the turon crisps up immediately and doesn’t absorb too much oil.
