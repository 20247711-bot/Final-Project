Afritada: The Flavor of the Fry

A savory Filipino stew simmered in rich tomato sauce.

Who Invented Afritada?

Afritada comes from the Spanish word “fritada”, meaning “fried,” referring to the step of frying the meat and aromatics before stewing.

No single person invented it — instead, it reflects how Filipinos adapted Spanish-style cooking to local ingredients like chicken, pork, and root vegetables.

Afritada vs Menudo vs Caldereta

  • Afritada: Large chicken or pork cuts, potatoes, carrots, bell peppers.
  • Menudo: Diced pork, liver, raisins, potatoes, carrots.
  • Caldereta: Beef or goat, liver spread, olives, cheese — richer and spicier.

A hearty tomato-based Filipino stew with vegetables and tender chicken.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs (1 kg) Chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 tbsp Vegetable oil
  • 1 Onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1 can Tomato sauce (8 oz)
  • 1 cup Water
  • 2 Potatoes, quartered
  • 2 Carrots, sliced
  • 1 Red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 Green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1/2 cup Green peas (optional)
  • 2 Bay leaves
  • Salt & Pepper

Instructions

  1. Sear: Brown chicken for 2–3 minutes.
  2. Sauté: Cook onions and garlic.
  3. Simmer: Add chicken, tomato sauce, water, and bay leaves.
  4. Add vegetables: Potatoes and carrots first.
  5. Finish: Add bell peppers and peas.
  6. Serve: Best paired with steaming hot rice.

TUTORIAL


Tip: Let the chicken simmer longer for a richer tomato flavor.
