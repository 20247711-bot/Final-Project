Pork Sisig: The Sizzling Filipino Favorite

A smoky, tangy, and savory dish made with pork and served on a sizzling plate.

Pork Sisig is one of the most beloved Filipino dishes, famous for its crispy, savory, and smoky flavors. Originally from Pampanga, this dish was created as a way to use leftover pig parts, but over time, it has evolved into a popular comfort food in the Philippines.

Sisig is typically made with pork (often the ears, cheeks, or liver), seasoned with calamansi (Filipino lime), soy sauce, and chili peppers, served on a sizzling plate for that extra crunch and heat. It's usually enjoyed with rice or as an appetizer.

A Flavor Explosion

The beauty of Pork Sisig lies in its combination of textures — crispy and tender pork, tangy from the calamansi, and spicy from the chili. It’s the ultimate Filipino comfort food that never fails to please.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb pork belly (or ears and cheeks), boiled and chopped
  • 1/2 lb chicken liver, chopped (optional)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 2-3 Thai bird's eye chilies (or to taste), chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 egg (optional, for extra richness)
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise (optional, for creaminess)
  • 1 tablespoon oil (for frying)
  • Sizzling plate or serving dish

How to Cook It

  1. Prepare the Pork: Boil the pork belly and/or liver until tender. Let it cool, then chop into small pieces.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat oil in a pan and sauté garlic and onion until fragrant and soft.
  3. Cook the Pork: Add the chopped pork and cook until crispy and browned. Season with soy sauce, vinegar, calamansi juice, and bird's eye chili.
  4. Add Optional Ingredients: Mix in mayonnaise and/or an egg if you prefer a creamier texture. Stir well to combine.
  5. Sizzle: Transfer the mixture to a sizzling plate and heat it on high to get a nice sizzle. Serve immediately!

TUTORIAL


Cooking Tip: For the perfect crispy texture, make sure to fry the pork bits until golden brown and crisp before adding them to the sizzling plate!
