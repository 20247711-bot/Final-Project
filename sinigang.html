Sinigang: The Soul of Sour

A warm, tangy Filipino comfort dish shaped by nature and tradition.

Sinigang has no single inventor. Its origins come from the culinary practices of early indigenous Filipinos who cooked meat and fish with naturally sour fruits.

Filipinos used whatever souring agents nature provided like tamarind, kamias, guava, green mango, and calamansi making Sinigang deeply tied to the geography of the islands.

A Dish Shaped by the Land

Because ingredients varied by region, Sinigang evolved into many versions. It became a dish defined by Filipino taste and environment instead of a single creator.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (500g) pork belly, cubed
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 3 pieces gabi (taro root), halved
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 bunch sitaw (long beans), cut into lengths
  • 1 pack tamarind soup mix or fresh tamarind
  • 1 bunch kangkong or mustard greens
  • 2 long green chilies
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish sauce and salt

How to Cook It

  1. Tenderize the Pork: Boil pork in water, skim foam, and simmer until tender.
  2. Add Aromatics: Add onion, tomatoes, and gabi. Simmer until gabi softens.
  3. Sour the Broth: Mix in tamarind soup base. Adjust sourness.
  4. Add Vegetables: Put in radish and sitaw. Simmer 5 minutes.
  5. Finish: Add chilies and greens until wilted.
  6. Serve: Season with patis and serve hot with rice.

TUTORIAL


*Cooking Tip:* Add gabi early to naturally thicken the soup for a richer broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack tamarind soup mix
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 bunch sitaw
  • 1 bunch kangkong or mustard greens
  • 2 long green chilies
  • 6 cups water
  • Fish sauce and salt

How to Cook It

  1. Boil Aromatics: Simmer onion and tomatoes until soft.
  2. Sour the Broth: Add tamarind soup mix and season.
  3. Add Vegetables: Add radish and sitaw.
  4. Add Shrimp: Add shrimp and chilies until shrimp turns pink.
  5. Finish: Add greens and turn off heat.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb fish (bangus, salmon head, or maya-maya)
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack tamarind soup mix
  • 1 radish, sliced
  • 1 bunch sitaw
  • 1 bunch kangkong or mustard greens
  • 2 long green chilies
  • 6 cups water
  • Ginger (optional)
  • Fish sauce and salt

How to Cook It

  1. Boil Aromatics: Simmer onion, tomatoes, and ginger.
  2. Sour the Broth: Add tamarind mix and season.
  3. Add Vegetables: Add radish and sitaw.
  4. Add Fish: Gently add fish and simmer.
  5. Finish: Add chilies and greens until wilted.

TUTORIAL


*Cooking Tip:* Add fish last to prevent it from breaking apart.
