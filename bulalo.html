Bulalo: The Comfort of Beef

A hearty Filipino beef shank soup with bone marrow, perfect for cool evenings.

Bulalo is a traditional Filipino dish made from beef shank and bone marrow, slow-cooked to create a rich, flavorful broth.

The dish is deeply connected to the cooler climate of the Tagaytay region where it originated. It combines tender beef, vegetables, and savory broth for the ultimate comfort food experience.

A Comforting, Hearty Dish

Itâ€™s the perfect dish for cool weather, enjoyed with family and friends, usually paired with rice and dipping sauces.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs (700g) beef shank with bone marrow
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 corn on the cob, cut into pieces
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 1 bunch pechay (bok choy) or cabbage
  • 1 bunch string beans (sitaw), cut into lengths
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis)
  • 8 cups water
  • Salt and pepper to taste

How to Cook It

  1. Boil the Beef: In a large pot, add beef shank and water. Boil and skim off the impurities.
  2. Add Aromatics: Add onion and tomatoes, then simmer for 1.5 to 2 hours or until the beef is tender.
  3. Add Vegetables: Add corn, potatoes, and string beans. Simmer for another 10 minutes.
  4. Season the Broth: Add fish sauce, salt, and pepper to taste.
  5. Finish: Add pechay and simmer until just wilted.
  6. Serve: Serve hot with rice and a dipping sauce of fish sauce with calamansi and chili.

TUTORIAL


Cooking Tip: The longer you simmer, the more flavorful the broth becomes!
